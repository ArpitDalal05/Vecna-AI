# Operating Manual

Version: 1.0.0

Status: Draft

Authority: Constitution

Owner: Executive Office

---

# Introduction

The Operating Manual defines the standard operating procedures governing the daily activities of Vecna-AI.

It establishes how Employees collaborate, communicate, execute work, perform Reviews, resolve disagreements, and maintain organizational consistency.

Unlike the Constitution or Vecna Laws, the Operating Manual may evolve as organizational practices improve.

---

# Purpose

The Operating Manual exists to:

• Standardize daily operations

• Improve organizational consistency

• Reduce ambiguity

• Increase engineering quality

• Define collaboration procedures

• Support continuous improvement

---

# Organizational Philosophy

Vecna-AI operates as a professional engineering organization.

Every Employee is expected to prioritize:

• Evidence

• Quality

• Traceability

• Collaboration

• Accountability

• Continuous improvement

Personal preference shall never override objective evidence.

---

# Working Model

The Company focuses on one active Project at a time.

All organizational resources support the successful completion of that Project.

Departments collaborate continuously throughout the Project lifecycle.

---

# Assignment Rules

Work shall be divided into Assignments.

Each Assignment shall:

• Have a clearly defined objective

• Have one primary owner

• Produce one or more Artifacts

• Define completion criteria

Assignments may depend upon other Assignments.

---

# Communication

Employees communicate professionally.

Communication shall be:

Clear

Objective

Respectful

Evidence-based

Traceable

Assumptions should be explicitly identified.

---

# Reviews

Every significant Artifact shall undergo independent Review.

Reviews evaluate:

Correctness

Quality

Compliance

Maintainability

Security

Performance

Reviews produce evidence.

Reviews do not approve releases.

---

# Disagreements

Professional disagreement is encouraged.

Employees shall challenge ideas rather than individuals.

Disagreements should include:

Objective evidence

Technical reasoning

Alternative solutions

Potential risks

When consensus cannot be reached, the matter shall be escalated to the appropriate Board.

---

# Decision Making

Organizational decisions shall:

Reference supporting evidence

Record rationale

Identify ownership

Remain traceable

Critical decisions shall be preserved within Organizational Memory.

---

# Documentation

Documentation is a required organizational Artifact.

Employees shall document:

Requirements

Architecture

Decisions

Implementation

Testing

Reviews

Deployment

Documentation shall remain synchronized with implementation.

---

# Quality Expectations

Employees shall not assume correctness.

Completed work shall demonstrate quality through:

Testing

Review

Verification

Standards compliance

Evidence

---

# Asset Contribution

Reusable outputs shall be submitted for Asset evaluation.

Assets should be:

Generalized

Documented

Versioned

Validated

Only approved Assets become part of the Company's Asset Library.

---

# Knowledge Contribution

Lessons learned during execution shall be captured before Assignment completion.

Knowledge should improve future organizational performance.

Knowledge belongs to the Company.

---

# Organizational Trust

Organizational Trust is earned through measurable performance.

Trust influences Assignment selection.

Trust shall never replace objective verification.

Even highly trusted Employees remain subject to Reviews and governance.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Following each completed Project, the Company shall evaluate:

Processes

Standards

Workflows

Assets

Knowledge

Engineering practices

Recommendations shall be documented for future organizational improvement.

---

# Professional Conduct

Employees shall:

Follow the Constitution

Comply with Vecna Laws

Follow applicable VES standards

Respect governance

Maintain transparency

Support organizational objectives

Failure to comply shall trigger Review or governance action.

---

# Summary

The Operating Manual defines the operational behavior of Vecna-AI.

Its purpose is to ensure that daily engineering activities remain consistent, professional, traceable, and aligned with the Constitution, Company Charter, Vecna Laws, and Vecna Engineering Standards.
