# Introduction

The Vecna Engineering Standards (VES) establish the mandatory engineering requirements governing software development within Vecna-AI.

VES documents define *how* engineering work shall be performed.

They translate the principles established by the Constitution, Company Charter, Vecna Laws, and Operating Manual into practical, enforceable engineering standards.

Compliance with VES is mandatory for all Employees.

---

# Purpose

The Vecna Engineering Standards exist to:

• Standardize engineering practices

• Improve software quality

• Ensure consistency

• Reduce engineering risk

• Preserve organizational knowledge

• Enable continuous improvement

---

# Scope

VES applies to all engineering activities performed by Vecna-AI.

Examples include:

• Research

• Requirements

• Architecture

• Design

• Implementation

• Documentation

• Testing

• Security

• Deployment

• Reviews

• Maintenance

No engineering activity is exempt from applicable VES standards.

---

# Engineering Principles

All VES documents shall support the following principles.

• Quality before speed

• Evidence before opinion

• Traceability by default

• Automation whenever practical

• Security by design

• Simplicity before complexity

• Reuse before reinvention

• Continuous improvement

# Requirement Levels

VES requirements use the following terminology.

**Shall**

A mandatory requirement.

Compliance is required.

---

**Should**

A recommended practice.

Exceptions require documented justification.

---

**May**

An optional practice.

Implementation depends upon organizational need.

---

# Compliance

Engineering work shall comply with every applicable VES document.

Non-compliance shall be documented, justified, reviewed, and approved where required.

Repeated non-compliance should trigger organizational review.

---

# Evolution

VES documents are living standards.

Organizational improvement

Every revision shall preserve traceability.
