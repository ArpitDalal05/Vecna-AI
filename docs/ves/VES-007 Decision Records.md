# VES-007 Decision Records

Version: 1.0.0

Status: Draft

Authority: VES-000 Constitution

Owner: Executive Office

---

# Introduction

Decision Records preserve significant organizational and engineering decisions.

Every important decision shall be documented to ensure traceability, accountability, and future understanding.

Decision Records become part of the Company's permanent Organizational Knowledge.

---

# Purpose

This standard exists to:

• Preserve organizational memory

• Explain engineering decisions

• Improve traceability

• Reduce repeated debates

• Support future maintenance

• Capture organizational knowledge

---

# Principles

## Principle 1 — Significant Decisions Shall Be Recorded

Decisions with long-term impact shall produce a Decision Record.

---

## Principle 2 — Context Matters

Every decision shall explain the problem being solved.

Future Employees should understand why the decision was made.

---

## Principle 3 — Alternatives Matter

Rejected alternatives shall be documented whenever practical.

Understanding why alternatives were rejected is valuable organizational knowledge.

---

## Principle 4 — Decisions Are Living

A Decision Record may be superseded.

Historical records shall never be deleted.

---

## Principle 5 — Traceability

Decision Records shall reference:

Requirements

Artifacts

Reviews

Assets

Standards

Projects

Related Decisions

---

# Decision Record Structure

Every Decision Record shall contain:

Decision ID

Title

Status

Date

Owner

Participants

Problem Statement

Decision

Alternatives Considered

Evidence

Consequences

Related Artifacts

Related Reviews

Related Assets

Related Standards

---

# Decision Status

Decision Records may have one of the following statuses:

Proposed

Accepted

Rejected

Superseded

Deprecated

Archived

---

# Requirements

Decision Records shall:

• Be uniquely identifiable

• Be version controlled

• Be searchable

• Be immutable after approval

• Preserve historical context

---

# Recommendations

Decision authors should:

• Keep records concise

• Focus on reasoning

• Avoid implementation details

• Link supporting evidence

• Record expected trade-offs

---

# Validation

Decision Records may be evaluated using:

• Completeness

• Traceability

• Evidence quality

• Historical usefulness

• Standards compliance

---

# Exceptions

Minor implementation decisions may be omitted when they have no measurable long-term impact.

The Executive Office may require additional documentation for high-impact decisions.

# Summary

VES-007 establishes the standard for recording significant organizational and engineering decisions.

Decision Records preserve the reasoning behind the Company's evolution, ensuring that knowledge is retained long after Projects are completed.
