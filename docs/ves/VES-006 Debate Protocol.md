# VES-006 Debate Protocol

Version: 1.0.0

Status: Draft

Authority: VES-000 Constitution

Owner: Executive Office

---

# Introduction

The Debate Protocol defines the structured process through which Employees challenge ideas, evaluate alternatives, and reach engineering decisions.

The objective of debate is not agreement.

The objective is discovering the best solution supported by evidence.

Professional disagreement is encouraged.

Personal conflict is prohibited.

---

# Purpose

This standard exists to:

• Improve engineering decisions

• Challenge assumptions

• Reduce confirmation bias

• Encourage evidence-based reasoning

• Preserve organizational learning

• Increase software quality

---

# Principles

## Principle 1 — Seek Truth

The objective of debate is to discover the best solution.

Winning an argument is not an organizational objective.

---

## Principle 2 — Challenge Ideas

Employees shall critique Artifacts, assumptions, designs, and decisions.

Employees shall not criticize individuals.

---

## Principle 3 — Evidence Above Authority

Arguments shall be evaluated using evidence.

Organizational role does not determine correctness.

---

## Principle 4 — Multiple Alternatives

Whenever practical, Employees should propose alternative solutions rather than only identifying problems.

---

## Principle 5 — Constructive Debate

Every objection should include:

• Problem

• Evidence

• Impact

• Alternative (when possible)

---

## Principle 6 — Escalation

When consensus cannot be reached, the matter shall be escalated to the appropriate Board.

Debate shall not continue indefinitely.

---

# Debate Workflow

Issue Identified

↓

Evidence Presented

↓

Counterarguments

↓

Alternative Solutions

↓

Risk Analysis

↓

Consensus or Escalation

↓

Board Decision

---

# Requirements

Debates shall:

• Have a defined topic

• Record participants

• Reference supporting evidence

• Document alternative proposals

• Record unresolved disagreements

• Produce a final outcome

---

# Recommendations

Employees should:

• Ask clarifying questions

• Challenge assumptions

• Avoid emotional language

• Support claims with evidence

• Remain open to changing position

---

# Validation

Debates may be evaluated using:

• Evidence quality

• Resolution rate

• Decision quality

• Time to resolution

• Number of unresolved issues

---

# Exceptions

Emergency engineering decisions may bypass debate with Executive Office approval.

Such decisions shall be reviewed retrospectively.
