# VES-009 Testing Standards

Version: 1.0.0

Status: Draft

Authority: VES-000 Constitution

Owner: Quality Assurance Department

---

# Introduction

The Testing Standards define the mandatory requirements for validating Artifacts produced by Vecna-AI.

Testing provides objective evidence that an Artifact satisfies its intended requirements.

Testing complements Reviews but does not replace them.

---

# Purpose

This standard exists to:

• Improve software quality

• Detect defects

• Verify requirements

• Reduce production failures

• Increase engineering confidence

• Support continuous improvement

---

# Principles

## Principle 1 — Evidence Through Testing

Correctness shall be demonstrated through objective validation.

Software shall not be assumed to be correct.

---

## Principle 2 — Early Testing

Testing should begin as early as practical within the engineering lifecycle.

---

## Principle 3 — Repeatability

Tests shall produce consistent and reproducible results.

---

## Principle 4 — Automation

Automated testing should be preferred whenever practical.

Manual testing should complement automation rather than replace it.

---

## Principle 5 — Risk-Based Testing

Higher-risk Artifacts shall receive proportionally greater testing effort.

---

## Principle 6 — Independence

Testing should be performed independently whenever practical.

Independent validation increases confidence.

---

## Principle 7 — Maintainability

Tests are engineering Artifacts.

They shall be readable, maintainable, and version controlled.

---

## Principle 8 — Continuous Improvement

Testing strategies shall evolve through organizational learning.

Recurring defects should influence future testing practices.

---

# Requirements

Engineering work shall:

• Define acceptance criteria.

• Validate functional requirements.

• Verify critical workflows.

• Record test outcomes.

• Preserve failed test evidence.

• Maintain test traceability.

• Prevent known regressions.

---

# Recommendations

Employees should:

• Automate repetitive tests.

• Test edge cases.

• Validate failure scenarios.

• Test realistic workloads.

• Keep tests isolated.

• Remove obsolete tests.

---

# Validation

Testing effectiveness may be evaluated using:

• Requirement Coverage

• Test Pass Rate

• Defect Detection Rate

• Escaped Defects

• Regression Rate

• Test Stability

• Automation Coverage

---

# Exceptions

Testing exceptions require documented approval.

Every exception shall include:

Reason

Risk

Mitigation

Approving Authority

Review Date
