# Assets

Version: 1.0.0

Status: Draft

Authority: Company Charter

Owner: Executive Office

---

# Introduction

Assets are reusable organizational resources that increase the Company's long-term capability.

Unlike Project Artifacts, Assets are intended for repeated use across future Projects.

Assets represent accumulated organizational value.

Every completed Project should increase the Company's Asset Library.

---

# Purpose

The Asset System exists to:

• Preserve valuable work

• Reduce duplicated effort

• Improve engineering quality

• Standardize organizational practices

• Accelerate future Projects

• Capture organizational investment

Assets are investments.

They are not temporary deliverables.

---

# Asset Definition

An Asset is any reusable organizational resource that provides measurable value beyond the Project in which it was created.

Assets shall be reusable, documented, versioned, and maintained.

---

# Asset Principles

Assets shall be:

Reusable

Versioned

Discoverable

Traceable

Validated

Maintained

Documented

Approved

Unused Assets should be periodically reviewed for relevance.

---

# Asset Ownership

Assets belong to the Company.

Departments maintain Assets on behalf of the Company.

Projects contribute Assets.

Employees create Assets.

Ownership remains organizational.

---

# Asset Quality

Assets shall satisfy:

Correctness

Maintainability

Documentation

Reusability

Standards Compliance

Version Control

Review Approval

Assets failing quality requirements shall not be published.

---

# Asset Library

The Company maintains a centralized Asset Library.

The Asset Library shall support:

Search

Categorization

Version History

Dependencies

Usage Tracking

Approval Records

Associated Standards

Every published Asset shall be uniquely identifiable.

---

# Asset Reuse

Employees shall prefer existing Assets before creating new ones.

Creating duplicate Assets should be avoided unless justified by measurable benefit.

Asset reuse contributes to organizational efficiency.

---

# Asset Retirement

Assets may be deprecated when:

Superseded

Obsolete

Technically incorrect

No longer maintained

Security concerns exist

Deprecated Assets remain historically accessible unless removed through governance.

---

# Asset Metrics

The Company shall monitor:

Reuse Rate

Approval Rate

Update Frequency

Maintenance Cost

Dependency Count

Project Adoption

Retirement Rate

These metrics support continuous improvement.

---

# Organizational Value

The Company's long-term value is reflected in the quality, maturity, and diversity of its Asset Library.

Assets transform completed work into permanent organizational capability.

---

# Summary

Assets represent the accumulated engineering capital of Vecna-AI.

By preserving reusable knowledge, software, templates, workflows, and standards, the Company continuously improves its ability to deliver higher quality results with greater consistency and efficiency.
