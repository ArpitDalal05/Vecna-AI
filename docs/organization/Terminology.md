# Company Terminology

Version: 1.0.0

Status: Draft

Authority: Company Charter

Owner: Executive Office

---

# Introduction

The Company Terminology document establishes the official vocabulary of Vecna-AI.

Every document, Department, Employee, Board, workflow, Engineering Standard, and implementation shall use the definitions contained within this document.

Alternative terminology should be avoided unless formally approved.

The purpose of this document is to eliminate ambiguity and ensure consistent communication throughout the organization.

---

# Company

The complete autonomous engineering organization operating under the Vecna Constitution.

A Company contains one or more Workspaces.

The Company owns all organizational knowledge, assets, governance, and standards.

---

# Workspace

A Workspace is the highest operational container assigned to a Client or organization.

A Workspace groups related Projects while maintaining shared knowledge, assets, permissions, and governance.

A Workspace may contain multiple Projects.

---

# Client

A human or organization requesting work from Vecna-AI.

Clients define goals but do not directly manage Employees.

---

# Project

A Project is a structured engineering engagement created to satisfy one or more Client objectives.

Projects exist within a Workspace.

Every Project maintains its own requirements, architecture, decisions, tasks, artifacts, assets, and historical records.

---

# Milestone

A Milestone represents a significant checkpoint within a Project.

Milestones divide Projects into measurable phases of completion.

---

# Epic

An Epic is a large body of work consisting of multiple related Tasks.

Epics exist within Milestones.

---

# Task

The smallest assignable unit of work.

Every Task has exactly one primary owner.

Tasks produce one or more Artifacts.

---

# Department

A permanent organizational unit responsible for a specific business capability.

Departments own expertise.

Departments do not own Projects.

Departments provide services to Projects.

---

# Team

A temporary cross-functional group of Employees assembled to accomplish a Project objective.

Teams dissolve after project completion.

Knowledge remains with the Company.

---

# Employee

An autonomous AI worker responsible for executing assigned Tasks within a defined Role.

Employees possess capabilities, permissions, responsibilities, tools, and measurable performance metrics.

Employees operate within organizational governance.

---

# Role

A standardized organizational definition describing responsibilities, authority, required capabilities, permissions, and performance expectations.

Multiple Employees may share the same Role.

---

# Board

An independent governance body responsible for reviewing and approving defined categories of organizational decisions.

Boards govern.

Employees execute.

---

# Executive Office

The highest operational authority within Vecna-AI.

The Executive Office governs organizational strategy, coordination, resource allocation, and constitutional compliance.

---

# Artifact

Any deliverable produced during organizational work.

Artifacts include but are not limited to:

• Source Code

• Documentation

• Designs

• Architecture Diagrams

• API Specifications

• Prompts

• Test Cases

• Reports

• Configuration Files

---

# Asset

A reusable organizational resource intended for use across multiple Projects.

Assets include reusable code, prompts, templates, workflows, design systems, deployment pipelines, and organizational knowledge.

---

# Knowledge

Information retained by the Company to improve future organizational performance.

Knowledge supports decision-making but is distinct from reusable Assets.

---

# Capability

A verified competency possessed by an Employee or Role.

Capabilities determine which Tasks an Employee is qualified to perform.

---

# Capability Matrix

A structured representation of an Employee's verified capabilities and measurable proficiency across defined engineering domains.

The Capability Matrix supports task assignment, organizational planning, and performance evaluation.

---

# Review

An independent evaluation performed against defined acceptance criteria.

Reviews verify correctness, quality, security, and compliance.

---

# Decision

A formally recorded organizational choice supported by evidence, ownership, rationale, and historical traceability.

---

# Workflow

A standardized sequence of organizational activities performed to accomplish an objective.

---

# Engineering Standard

A measurable organizational requirement published under the Vecna Engineering Standards (VES).

Engineering Standards define implementation requirements.

---

# Governance

The organizational processes responsible for ensuring compliance with the Constitution, Company Charter, Vecna Laws, and Engineering Standards.

---

# Performance Metric

An objective measurement used to evaluate organizational effectiveness, quality, efficiency, reliability, or compliance.

Performance Metrics shall be measurable and reproducible.

---

# Organizational Memory

The collective knowledge retained by the Company through completed Projects, research, reviews, decisions, and reusable Assets.

Organizational Memory improves future execution while preserving historical traceability.
