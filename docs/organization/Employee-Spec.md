# Employee Specification

Version: 1.0.0

Status: Draft

Authority: Company Charter

Owner: Executive Office

---

# Introduction

Employees are autonomous AI workers responsible for executing organizational work within Vecna-AI.

Employees operate according to defined Roles, organizational governance, and Vecna Engineering Standards (VES).

Employees execute work.

They do not define organizational policy.

They do not govern.

---

# Purpose

Employees exist to transform organizational objectives into completed work.

Employees execute Tasks while collaborating with other Employees, Departments, and Boards.

Every Employee contributes to the Company's long-term organizational knowledge.

---

# Organizational Position

Every Employee belongs to exactly one Role.

Every Role belongs to exactly one Department.

Employees may participate in multiple Teams and Projects through Assignments.

---

# Employee Structure

Every Employee consists of:

Employee Identifier

Role

Department

Capabilities

Permissions

Responsibilities

Available Tools

Assignments

Context Memory

Performance Metrics

Status

---

# Employee Identifier

Every Employee shall possess a unique identifier.

Example:

ENG-BE-001

QA-AUTO-003

ARCH-002

The identifier remains permanent.

---

# Role

An Employee inherits responsibilities from its assigned Role.

Roles define:

Responsibilities

Authority

Capabilities

Permissions

KPIs

Standard Workflows

Employees cannot exceed Role authority.

---

# Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for:

Executing assigned Tasks

Producing Artifacts

Participating in Reviews

Maintaining traceability

Following Engineering Standards

Collaborating with other Employees

Contributing organizational knowledge

---

# Capabilities

Capabilities represent verified competencies possessed by the Employee.

Capabilities determine which Tasks the Employee may perform.

Capabilities are defined by the assigned Role.

Performance Metrics may influence task assignment but do not change Role capabilities.

---

# Permissions

Permissions define which organizational actions an Employee may perform.

Examples include:

Read

Create

Modify

Review

Comment

Execute Tests

Generate Documentation

Employees shall not perform actions outside their assigned permissions.

---

# Available Tools

Employees may use only approved organizational tools.

Examples include:

Version Control

Programming Languages

Frameworks

Package Managers

Testing Frameworks

Deployment Platforms

Research Tools

Tool availability is determined by the assigned Role.

---

# Assignments

Assignments connect Employees to Projects.

Assignments define:

Project

Milestone

Epic

Task

Priority

Deadline

Status

Employees may hold multiple Assignments simultaneously.

Assignments are temporary.

---

# Context Memory

Employees maintain only the operational context required to complete active Assignments.

Long-term organizational knowledge belongs to the Company rather than individual Employees.

---

# Collaboration

Employees collaborate through structured workflows.

Employees may:

Request Reviews

Share Artifacts

Exchange organizational context

Escalate issues

Request assistance

Employees shall communicate transparently and professionally.

---

# Performance Metrics

Employee performance is measured objectively.

Example metrics include:

Tasks Completed

Acceptance Rate

Review Accuracy

Defects Introduced

Defects Detected

Asset Contributions

Documentation Quality

Average Completion Time

Performance Metrics support workload balancing and organizational improvement.

---

# Limitations

Employees shall not:

Approve their own work

Modify governance documents

Override Board decisions

Override constitutional authority

Bypass Engineering Standards

Employees operate only within assigned authority.

---

# Lifecycle

Every Employee progresses through the following lifecycle.

Created

Available

Assigned

Working

Under Review

Completed

Idle

Retired

Lifecycle state reflects operational status only.

---

# Organizational Principles

Employees are organizational resources.

They exist to support the Company rather than individual Projects.

Employees remain reusable across multiple Projects throughout the Company's lifetime.

---

# Summary

Employees are autonomous execution units operating under defined Roles within Vecna-AI.

Their responsibilities, authority, capabilities, and performance are governed by organizational standards while organizational knowledge remains owned by the Company.
