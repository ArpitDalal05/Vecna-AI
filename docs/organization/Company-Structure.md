# Company Structure

Version: 1.0.0

Status: Draft

Authority: Company Charter

Owner: Executive Office

---

# Introduction

The Company Structure defines the organizational hierarchy of Vecna-AI.

Its purpose is to establish clear reporting relationships, authority boundaries, communication paths, and organizational responsibilities.

Every Employee, Department, Team, Board, and Project shall operate within this structure.

No organizational unit may exist outside the Company Structure.

---

# Organizational Principles

The Company Structure is designed according to the following principles.

• Clear authority

• Defined accountability

• Independent governance

• Cross-functional collaboration

• Organizational scalability

• Knowledge preservation

---

# Organizational Hierarchy

The Company is organized into the following hierarchy.

Company

↓

Workspace

↓

Project

↓

Milestone

↓

Epic

↓

Task

↓

Artifact

This hierarchy represents the operational flow of work performed by the organization.

---

# Executive Office

The Executive Office is the highest operational authority within Vecna-AI.

The Executive Office governs the organization but does not perform implementation work.

Responsibilities include:

• Organizational governance

• Strategic planning

• Resource allocation

• Organizational health

• Constitutional compliance

• Conflict escalation

• Department coordination

---

# Departments

Departments are permanent organizational units responsible for maintaining expertise in a defined business capability.

Departments own capabilities.

Departments do not own Projects.

Departments provide services to Projects.

Each Department maintains:

• Mission

• Responsibilities

• Employees

• Assets

• Knowledge Domains

• KPIs

---

# Teams

Teams are temporary groups created to accomplish Project objectives.

Teams consist of Employees from one or more Departments.

A Team exists only while its assigned objectives remain active.

Upon completion:

• Tasks are closed

• Knowledge is retained

• Assets are catalogued

• Team is dissolved

---

# Employees

Employees execute organizational work.

Employees belong to exactly one Department.

Employees may participate in multiple Teams.

Employees never belong permanently to Projects.

---

# Boards

Boards provide independent governance.

Boards review organizational decisions.

Boards approve or reject work according to their authority.

Boards never implement.

Boards remain organizationally independent from implementation Departments.

---

# Workspaces

A Workspace represents the highest operational container for a Client or organization.

A Workspace contains one or more Projects sharing common governance, Assets, and organizational knowledge.

Workspaces provide isolation between Clients while enabling collaboration within related Projects.

---

# Projects

Projects exist inside Workspaces.

A Project represents a defined engineering engagement.

Projects consume organizational capabilities but do not permanently own Employees.

Projects produce:

• Requirements

• Decisions

• Artifacts

• Assets

• Organizational Knowledge

---

# Authority Flow

Authority flows downward through the organization.

Constitution

↓

Company Charter

↓

Executive Office

↓

Departments

↓

Teams

↓

Employees

Operational authority may never contradict constitutional authority.

---

# Communication Flow

Communication follows organizational responsibilities.

Employee ↔ Employee

↓

Team

↓

Department

↓

Board (when governance is required)

↓

Executive Office (when escalation is required)

Communication should remain transparent, traceable, and evidence-based.

---

# Organizational Independence

Governance and implementation shall remain independent.

Employees implement.

Boards govern.

Departments provide expertise.

The Executive Office coordinates.

No organizational unit may simultaneously create, approve, and release the same critical Artifact.

---

# Organizational Assets

Assets belong to the Company.

Departments maintain Assets.

Projects consume Assets.

Completed Projects contribute new Assets back to the Company.

The Company's Asset Library continuously expands through organizational work.

---

# Organizational Knowledge

Knowledge belongs to the Company.

Knowledge is preserved beyond the lifetime of Projects, Teams, or Employees.

Departments may specialize in knowledge domains, but ownership remains organizational.

---

# Scalability

The Company Structure is designed to support:

• Multiple Clients

• Multiple Workspaces

• Multiple simultaneous Projects

• Hundreds of Employees

• New Departments

• Additional Boards

without requiring structural redesign.

---

# Summary

The Company Structure establishes a scalable organizational model that separates governance, execution, knowledge, and authority while enabling autonomous collaboration across all levels of Vecna-AI.
