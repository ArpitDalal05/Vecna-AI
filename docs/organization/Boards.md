Status: Draft

Authority: Company Charter

---

# Introduction

Boards are independent governance bodies responsible for reviewing, approving, rejecting, or escalating organizational decisions within Vecna-AI.

Boards ensure that engineering decisions satisfy constitutional principles, organizational policies, and Vecna Engineering Standards (VES).

Boards govern.

Employees execute.

Departments provide expertise.

---

# Purpose

Boards exist to:

• Ensure independent review

• Maintain engineering quality

• Enforce organizational standards

• Prevent conflicts of interest

• Reduce organizational risk

• Preserve long-term consistency

Boards exist to make decisions, not to perform implementation.

---

# Organizational Principles

Boards operate according to the following principles.

• Independence

• Evidence-based evaluation

• Accountability

• Transparency

• Traceability

• Constitutional compliance

---

# Board Authority

Boards possess authority only within their defined governance domain.

Boards may:

• Approve work

• Reject work

• Request modifications

• Escalate organizational issues

• Record formal decisions

Boards may not:

• Implement work

• Modify Artifacts directly

• Bypass the Constitution

• Override another Board outside their authority

---

# Board Membership

A Board consists of one or more Employees assigned governance responsibilities.

Every Board shall define:

• Purpose

• Authority

• Scope

• Decision Criteria

• Voting Rules

• Escalation Rules

• Outputs

Membership may change without changing the Board's responsibilities.

---

# Decision Categories

Boards review decisions appropriate to their authority.

Decision categories include:

• Strategic

• Architectural

• Operational

• Governance

Each decision shall be recorded with its category, rationale, evidence, owner, and outcome.

# Standard Boards

Vecna-AI initially defines the following Boards.

• Architecture Board

• Security Board

• Quality Board

• Research Board

• Release Board

• Governance Board

Additional Boards may be created through organizational governance.

---

# Decision Outcomes

A Board may produce one of the following outcomes.

Approved

Rejected

Approved with Conditions

Deferred

Escalated

Every outcome shall include documented reasoning.

---

# Evidence Requirements

Board decisions shall be supported by objective evidence whenever reasonably possible.

Evidence may include:

• Test Results

• Benchmarks

• Research Findings

• Risk Assessments

• Compliance Reports

• Review Reports

• Metrics

Opinions alone shall not justify a governance decision.

---

# Voting

Boards should seek consensus whenever possible.

When consensus cannot be achieved, the Board shall follow its documented voting procedure.

Dissenting opinions may be recorded for historical traceability.

---

# Escalation

A Board shall escalate matters when:

• Constitutional interpretation is required

• Multiple Boards have conflicting authority

• Organizational policy is unclear

• Significant organizational risk exists

Escalated matters are resolved by the Executive Office.

---

# Independence

No Employee shall approve work they directly created.

Board members participating in implementation shall recuse themselves from reviewing that specific work.

Independent governance shall always be preserved.

---

# Transparency

Every Board decision shall produce a permanent organizational record containing:

Decision Identifier

Decision Category

Artifact(s) Reviewed

Evidence

Outcome

Rationale

Board Members

Timestamp

Related Project

This record becomes part of the Company's Organizational Memory.

---

# Performance Metrics

Boards should continuously evaluate their effectiveness.

Example metrics include:

Review Accuracy

Decision Consistency

Average Review Time

Escalation Rate

Reopened Decisions

Standards Compliance

Organizational Trust

---

# Summary

Boards provide independent governance across Vecna-AI.

They ensure that organizational decisions remain objective, evidence-based, transparent, and compliant with the Constitution, Company Charter, Vecna Laws, and Vecna Engineering Standards.
